### **My Journey with Kanji: A Tale of Dedication and Discovery**

#### **The Beginning: A Visual Approach**

When I first embarked on my journey to master the Japanese kanji, my strategy was straightforward – rely on my visual memory and quickly associate mnemonics with each character. This method seemed efficient, allowing me to cover a lot of ground rapidly. I was learning an impressive 50 new kanji every day, dedicating 2-4 hours to this task alongside my school and other activities. The goal was clear: complete the Joyo kanji set, a feat that requires both dedication and discipline.

#### **A Realization: The Limits of Surface Learning**

However, as I progressed, I noticed something crucial. Despite my rapid pace, my retention was often superficial. The kanji I learned quickly were also quickly forgotten, lasting only a few days in my memory. This realization hit me hard – I was not truly engaging with the kanji. My method was akin to skimming the surface of a vast ocean without diving deep to discover its treasures.

#### **Shifting Gears: Deepening the Mnemonic Connection**

This epiphany led me to change my approach. I began to focus more on the mnemonics, associating them deeply with the meanings of each kanji. This required more energy, focus, and time – sometimes significantly more than my initial 20 seconds per kanji. But the results were immediately noticeable. My brain was more engaged, and the kanji started to stick in my memory with a firmer grip.

#### **The Challenge: Balancing Visual Memory and Mnemonics**

One of my biggest challenges has been my strong visual memory. While it’s an asset in many ways, it sometimes hinders my ability to recall the mnemonics. I often find the visual memory giving me the answer faster than I can recall the mnemonic. It's like having a double-edged sword – a 'too good' short-term visual memory that interferes with long-term mnemonic recall.

#### **Rethinking and Relearning: Strengthening Long-term Memory**

Now, I'm considering revisiting some kanji, especially those I learned more visually. The idea is to relearn them with a deeper focus on mnemonics to ensure they stick in my long-term memory. It's a challenging task, given the sheer number of characters I've already covered, but it seems necessary for true mastery.

#### **Nearing the Milestone: Reflecting on the Journey**

As of today, I am nearing the completion of the Joyo kanji, with just 250 characters left. This milestone, just five days away at my current pace, marks not just an achievement in learning but also a testament to my dedication and effort.

#### **Looking Forward: Continual Learning and Balance**

What I’ve learned about myself and the process of learning kanji is invaluable. The journey has been intense, demanding almost all of my free time. But as I near its end, I realize that this is just one phase of a lifelong journey in language learning. The key is not just hard work but also finding the most effective strategies that work for me while maintaining a balance with my overall well-being.

As I move forward, I carry with me the lessons learned, ready to apply them in my continued exploration of the Japanese language and its beautifully complex writing system.
